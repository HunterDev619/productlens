## Environmental Impact Overview

This comprehensive lifecycle assessment reveals that the SCDF Forward Command Vehicle generates approximately **279,100 kg CO2e** over its 10-year lifespan, positioning it as a *high* impact product within the heavy-duty emergency vehicle category.

### Lifecycle Emission Distribution
The **use phase** dominates the environmental footprint, contributing **68%** of total emissions, primarily due to high diesel fuel consumption during operations (189,000 kg CO2e). This is followed by raw materials at **25%** (69,400 kg CO2e from metal extraction) and manufacturing at **5%** (15,000 kg CO2e).

### Critical Environmental Indicators
- **Carbon Intensity:** 279,100 kg CO2e per vehicle unit
- **Water Footprint:** 120,000 liters consumed across lifecycle
- **Material Efficiency:** 60% recyclable content, 75% recovery potential
- **Energy Demand:** 1,200,000 kWh total energy requirement (mostly fossil-based)

### Sustainability Performance
Compared to industry benchmarks, this product performs **20% above** the category average for diesel trucks. Key environmental concerns include high GHG emissions from use and resource depletion from aluminum/copper sourcing. However, positive aspects include strong metal recyclability and European manufacturing with moderate grid intensity.

### Improvement Pathways
Immediate opportunities for emission reduction include:
1. **Electrification of powertrain** with potential 50% reduction in use phase emissions.
2. **Recycled material sourcing** yielding 15,000 kg CO2e savings in raw materials.
3. **Fuel efficiency upgrades** improving operational efficiency by 20%, saving 37,800 kg CO2e.

*Note: Assessment conducted per ISO 14040/14044 standards using IPCC AR6 emission factors and regional grid intensities.*
